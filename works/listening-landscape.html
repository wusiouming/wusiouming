Sound Installation / Generative System • 2023

Concept

Listening Landscape approaches landscape as something that can be heard rather than only seen. The work translates the relations, emotions, and memories between people and site into sound, allowing place to emerge as a sonic field rather than a fixed image.

Developed from the artist’s long-term attention to environment, perception, and spatial listening, the project uses field recordings and image analysis as a method of reading place. Urban and rural textures are treated as shifting scores, where environmental conditions are continually recomposed through sound.

Through a custom program, visual data is decomposed into color and tonal values that generate a slowly changing sonic field. The work does not present a closed composition; instead, it opens a listening situation in which audience members can encounter the memory, rhythm, and tension embedded within the landscape.
