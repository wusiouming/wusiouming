Concept

Listen to the Breath of the City is an ongoing sound-based project that explores how we perceive and emotionally inhabit urban environments. The city breathes, but we rarely notice it. Between buildings, streets, and transitional spaces, subtle variations of density, openness, and rhythm shape our psychological state, and this project attempts to make those invisible conditions perceptible.

Through a custom-built system, images of urban spaces captured by the artist or participants are analyzed and translated into generative sound. The density of greenery and spatial openness are mapped into breathing patterns: compressed, rapid breaths for dense concrete environments, and slow, expanded breaths for more open or green spaces. Presented as a networked system, the project invites visitors to contribute images from their own surroundings through a web-based interface, allowing individual perceptions to accumulate into a collective rhythm.
