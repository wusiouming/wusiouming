Records of Landscape

Interactive Sound Installation • 2023

Concept

Records of Landscape explores the intersection of landscape, perception, local experience, and listening, re-examining the landscapes and human experiences of declining areas through new technologies. This work originates from the artist’s long-term project “Surrounding Spectrum,” which began with a deep concern for place and an exploration of landscapes.

Since 2018, the artist has conducted extensive field research in several rural towns, observing how social change reshapes places—military villages that could not be preserved, decaying old business districts, and small towns affected by population migration. Seeking a new perspective within these disappearing textures, the artist translates space and memory into sound by developing a computational system that converts landscape imagery into generative music. The system analyzes video data in real time, transforming it into musical compositions and compiling them into a sound booklet alongside local stories, forming a new kind of sound cartography.

Published in October 2023, the work focuses on remote villages surrounding the artist’s city, using drones to capture local imagery and AI-based computation to enrich the musical readings of the landscapes. The final presentation is a sound installation: when visitors approach the turntable, a sensor triggers the record to spin and play music generated from the landscapes, allowing audiences to experience these overlooked places through sound.
