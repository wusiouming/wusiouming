Biography, exhibitions, awards

Wu Siou Ming, based in Kaohsiung, Taiwan, is a sound artist and interdisciplinary creator whose practice spans sound, digital media, interactive systems, and installation. His work investigates how technological environments shape human perception, temporality, and collective experience, situating artistic practice within broader social and cultural infrastructures. Operating across both physical and online spaces, Wu develops participatory systems that transform everyday data, bodily gestures, and environmental signals into evolving audiovisual compositions. These processes critically examine how digital infrastructures influence attention, behavior, and modes of relation, while questioning systemic power structures and the cultural conditions of contemporary digital life. Wu’s works have been presented in museums and international festivals across Asia, Europe, and the Americas. He has received the Don Sante Prize for Contemporary Art – Social Art Award (Italy), the Arte Laguna Prize Special Award, and the Kaohsiung Award, and was selected for Taiwan’s Ministry of Culture “Made in Taiwan” program. His projects have been showcased at Ars Electronica, SIGGRAPH Asia, Science Gallery Monterrey, Rencontres Internationales Paris/Berlin, Museum of Science Boston, and the Athens Biennale. By integrating sound, generative systems, and interactive media, Wu creates poetic yet critical experiences that invite audiences to reconsider their relationship with technology, environment, and collective memory.
